Personalized Learning Designer
  • Type: LMS
  • Client: Open LMS
  • Role: UI/UX Designer
  • PLD
OVERVIEW

The Personalized Learning Designer is a powerful tool that can automate many processes of classroom management

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The Personalized Learning Designer is one of the best differentiators on the platform. We decided to redesign the Personalized Learning Designer. To keep consistency in the platform and making a simpler design and user focus.

BACKGROUND
HYPOTHESIS

- Keep consistency in the platform. - Having steps to help the users in the process. - Structure the content, organizing the information.

BUSINESS OPPORTINITIES

By redesigning this app, making it more modern and by being one of the biggest assets in the platform we enhance the use and fixing by elements that can help the experience of our user, making one of the biggest tools more appealing.

DESIGN PROCESS

1. Empathize : Research.
2. Define : User Persona, User Journey Mapping.
3. Ideate : Branding design.
4. Build : Wireframes.
5. Test : Brand Page and Final UI Screens.

EMPATHIZE

RESEARCH

After conducting one on one meetings to discover the interaction in the personalized learning designer we came to the conclusion.

  • How long do you expect the automatization to take?

  • Under what circumstances would you want to receive an alert in import a process?

  • What was your overall impression of creating a rule?

  • What was the best/worst thing about of creating a rule?

  • How would you change the tool?

Insights

This is a great program. The teaching-learning process becomes more effective

  • Steps are not reflected with tabs, the tabs content is small, we can let one page with steps instead of tabs Historial and rule steps and filter

  • I love this tool because it like putting your course on auto-pilot.

  • It seems to me an essential tool to give life to the courses. Unfortunately, few of our clients decide to use it, perhaps due to lack of time or knowledge.

  • Lack of feedback while or after creating a rule. Rule name, add and save are all over can relate to the content.

  • Overall Design aspects such as: Responsive, alignment, group and hierarchy. Editing and deleting elements are too small.
